Cairo Metro

Cairo, Egypt

Cairo Metro is Egypt’s rapid transit system and the first of its kind in Africa. It serves the capital city, Cairo, and some surrounding areas with a fast, affordable, and efficient means of transportation. The metro currently has three main lines that connect major districts and landmarks, easing daily commutes and reducing traffic congestion. Trains are typically air-conditioned, and each train includes a designated car for women for added safety and comfort. The system is continually expanding to accommodate the city's growing population and urban development.

Features

  • 1. Station Accessibility
    • -Elevators (Lifts): Available in main interchange stations like Sadat and Ataba to support movement between levels.
    • -Ramps: Present in selected stations to allow smooth access for wheelchair users.
    • -Tactile Paths: Installed in some stations to assist visually impaired passengers in navigating safely.
  • 2. Train Accessibility
    • -Wheelchair Spaces: Some newer trains have areas for wheelchair users.
    • -Manual Boarding Assistance: Station staff can provide help for boarding and getting off the train.
    • -Station Announcements: Both audio and visual announcements are available in most trains for next station alerts.
  • 3. Additional Services
    • -Priority Seating: Seats marked for elderly, pregnant women, and passengers with disabilities.
    • -Security Assistance: Staff available in major stations to support passengers with special needs.
    • -Service Hotlines: Available for travel assistance and reporting accessibility issues.
